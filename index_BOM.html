Step 1: Print parts

Using a FDM 3D printer loaded with at least 200 g of polypropylene filament, print two cell bodies with barbed fittings and two flow frames.

Struggling to print watertight, dimensionally accurate polypropylene parts?

You can also fabricate these parts by milling them from a larger sheet of polypropylene

Step 2: Post-process printed parts

  1. Remove brim, raft, support material from print.
  2. Sand down, with fine-grit sandpaper:
    1. The sealing faces of each cell body
    2. Both faces of each flow frame
  3. For each cell body, secure it in a vise and drill out the alignment pin holes of the cell bodies to their final diameter with a drill and an 1/8" drill bit

Caution

Depending on the dimensional accuracy of your printed parts, you may need to additionally drill out the electrolyte inlet/outlet holes to the correct diameter with an appropriately sized drill bit

This will produce two cell bodies and two flow frames.

Step 3: Cut gaskets

Note:

Cutting the gaskets is most easily done with a vinyl cutter machine, but can also be done manually with a steady hand, utility blade, and appropriately sized punches.

  1. Using a vinyl cutter machine, cut a sheet of gasket material to make:
    • one pass-through gasket

    • three blocking gaskets

This will produce one pass-through gasket and three blocking gaskets.

Step 4: Cut alignment pins

Using a tubing cutter, cut two pieces of 1/8" outside diameter hard plastic tubing into 5 cm lengths.

This will produce two alignment pins, which should fit smoothly in to the alignment pin holes in the cell bodies.
